Mrs Cassidy

The elusive geography teacher and Deputy Head Mrs Cassidy joined BRGS in the 2014/15 school year. She was well known for being one of the most smartly dressed teachers and seemed to have a different blazer for each day of the year.

A Typical Lesson

Her lessons mostly comprised of letting around half the students in the class (always the same half) wander around school with a compass and map, or looking for signs of weathering etc. whilst she taught the other half from a textbook and PowerPoint. One time we had to look up the cost of transport on laptops for whatever reason and we ended up reserving spaces on a ferry that left later during that lesson.

Mrs Cassidy may have been one of the only teachers who actually didn't mind when people talked during class and didn't enforce silence during her lessons. She will be missed - although she did say she would make a return to visit 16S during their Speech Night but we are yet to see if she will actually turn up!

Current Whereabouts

Mrs Cassidy, clearly believing being Deputy Head of BRGS was not enough, left at the end of the 2016/17 school year and has now gone on to being Headteacher at Crossley Heath Grammar School. This is probably because CHGS is a much nicer school and definitely one ups BRGS' "belfries" as it has its own clock tower and the bannisters have little casts of lions every few inches. CHGS is also in Yorkshire.

Unfortunately, the promotion has led to a personality shift and many students at Crossleys complain that she doesn't let teachers put on films before Christmas etc.
