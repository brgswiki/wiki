Mrs Porteous is Head of Year 11 and a biology teacher. She is also wife to none other than Mr Porteous himself. One time she let us watch Twilight because it has that one 30 second scene about the science teacher talking about mitosis in the background and so therefore was considered educational.

The Porteous Family

To anyone who has experience more than one lesson with her, you could probably write a book about the Porteii family, as most lessons are spent discussing anecdotes that loosely link to the topic (for example, when telling us how to write a six marker, Mrs P instead told us her mum's recipe for cheese sauce and how she came across it). Of course, no one really minds these stories as they are a wonderful way to relieve the amount of biology. After hearing about the Porteous Family through many recounts, it can be concluded that there are many similarities with the Addams Family. And although Alan is no Gomez Addams, the sheer size of extended family can be compared to that of the Addams clan, and the stories behind them all.

The Porteous Curse

Following the theme, during the Year 10 disease topic, it was discovered that many of the people Mrs Porteous knew had died strange or unusual deaths... Acquaintances of Mrs Porteous you have been warned...

The Next David Attenborough?

During the lockdown, when we were studying plant defence mechanisms, Mrs P filmed around the garden with a running commentary - and although she herself says that it was "not quite up to Attenborough standard" it certainly wasn't far off. Some highlights of these videos include:

  • "You can just imagine a little fox being able to put its paw up in those flowers."
  • "We have a vivacious weed here."
  • "I'm going to be very wary as I touch this plant." grabs nettle plant "Ouch." continues to hold the plant.
  • "These are stinging nettles, and I can tell you now, if you had a mouth full of that you wouldn't be back in a hurry for more."
  • "Looks harmless enough... until you get up to the stems."
  • "Now we're up in the wild part of my garden. Basically up in my weed patch."
  • "Now this is a very interesting plant."
  • "We just have this lush green growth here."
  • "You have the sharp thorns on the leaf here... get that in your mouth and you'll know about it."
